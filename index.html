Bloomington, Indiana


Bloomington is a small college town. What it lacks in size it more than makes up in culture and
events. In my time there I attended a political film viewing and documentry viewing for free in a local
bar. I also visited the University and ate at some fantastic restaurants. The town is very hip due to
its college aged population.

Chicago, Illinois


Chicago is with out a doubt one of my favorite cities in the whole world. It's full of big city appeal
while also having many hidden jem neighborhoods spead out through out all of the area. It's a great mix of
culture, touresty stuff and beautiful architecture. It's hard to choose specific areas but for me Logan square, pilsen and Downtown
are essentials and must hit areas. Don't miss out on Antique Taco, Emporium arcade bar, Fields museum, and the art institute!

Seattle, Washington


Seattle is a very special city, It's unlike most larger cities. It got all the tell tale big city perks
such as great culture, night life and happenings but it just has a different vibe. It has a a fantastic outdoors market
named Pike place market where you can get anything from fresh produce to odd crafts and gadgets. The weather for the most part
as expected, overcast and rainy but I expierienced a few sunny days so it's not all gloom. Also the city includes a front row seat
to mt rainier, a large still very active volcano. Plus starbucks, if you're into that sort of thing.

Eugene, Oregon


Eugene is unlike any other place on this list because it's not really known for anything in particular.
What I enjoyed about it was its small town feel, community and all around beautiful landscape. Like most of Oregon
it is surrounded by beautiful trees and woods. It's more of a place I'd see myself settling down in in my older age.
Also one of the perks of Oregon is no sales tax and in Eugene last I checked there was no Walmart! So that alone
is fairly unique.

Red wood forest


The Red wood forest is without a doubt one of the most breath taking sites I have ever seen. These mamoth trees range somewhere
between 200-300 feet but always vary in size and have grown much taller. This National park is a must and I ighly recoment
camping here. It's free and there are spots overlooking the Pacific Ocean that are stunning. The weather is beautiful for hiking
in the summer months. Words cannot describe what it's like seeing one of these goliaths in person so dont skip it!

San Fransisco, California

San Fransisco is the ultimate hip town, It has amazing weather, huge hills and a college campus
in the middle of the city. It has wonderful bars, record stores and just all around amazing food. Very friendly
commnity centers with lots of resources and things to expierience while there. While the city is pricey it is
totally one of the coolest cities in the US and the golden gate bridge is a site to behold.

Denver, Colorado

Denver and Colorado in general is a just beautiful from start to finish. With it's breathtaking mountain ranges
to it's laid back and relax atmostphere it is with out a doubt one of the must see cities in the US.
If you enjoy the outdoors, the cold and natural beauty then Colorado is for you. Denver is a great city with
fantastic night life and happenings. Worth the drive up the mountains!
